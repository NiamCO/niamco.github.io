About the Author

I am a young writer with a strong interest in speculative and dystopian fiction. I enjoy building rule-based worlds and exploring how systems affect individual behavior, especially from a youth perspective.

Unlisted is my first completed novel-length manuscript. I am currently continuing to develop my writing craft through revision, long-form storytelling, and focused feedback.

My writing focuses on surveillance societies, systemic control, and the resilience of individuals within oppressive structures. I'm fascinated by how systems adapt to maintain order and how human unpredictability challenges even the most sophisticated mechanisms of control.

Published Works

Unlisted

Completed Manuscript

In a tightly regulated society where children are monitored, categorized, and granted privileges strictly by age, thirteen-year-old Alex lives as a Pre-Citizen under constant surveillance.

Synopsis

One ordinary school day, Alex's identity record vanishes from the system entirely. Without explanation or warning, Alex becomes "Unlisted," officially nonexistent in a world that depends on constant tracking.

At first, Alex survives by blending in, pretending nothing has changed. Soon, they encounter Mara and other Unlisted kids who live in hidden corridors, abandoned maintenance routes, and forgotten rooms within the school and city infrastructure. These children have learned to move unseen, avoiding patrol drones and system scans while relying on memorized routes and shared knowledge. The system, however, is not blind. It is adaptive.

As security measures tighten, Alex learns that the surveillance system has begun studying anomalies rather than erasing them immediately. Alex's behavior, resilience, and ability to adapt without instruction draw the system's attention. Instead of removing Alex, the system begins observing them closely, treating Alex as a variable rather than a threat.

When several Unlisted kids disappear after being flagged as unstable variables, Alex realizes that invisibility is no longer protection. The system is learning how to eliminate deviation without collapse. Alex becomes a focal point of that learning.

Faced with the choice to remain hidden or act, Alex works with the Unlisted network to disrupt key access points and expose blind spots in the system's logic. During a final confrontation, Alex deliberately forces the system to overcommit its resources, proving that human unpredictability cannot be fully modeled or controlled.

The system responds by restoring Alex's record, attempting to reintegrate them as a compliant citizen. Alex refuses. Instead, Alex escapes with the remaining Unlisted, leaving the system damaged but operational, aware that it cannot fully control what it cannot understand.

The novel ends with the system still in place, but changed, and Alex choosing life outside its structure, knowing resistance is ongoing rather than complete.
